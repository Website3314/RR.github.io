Why having clear skin doesnt matter

And by clear face I mean the usual, no whiteheads, blackheads, pimples, hyperpigmentation etc. This dreaded trend which is a necessity for the modern ideal woman, is rapidly (and thankfully, with respect to gender equality) becoming a requirement even for the modern ideal man.

And yes I am calling this a trend because just like every other trend, its just something a celebrity or some old guy started probably a 100 years ago and the vast majority considered having a clear face, clean and appealing so they began following it (predictable), the remaining minority that thought this was bullshit eventually succumbed and fell in line. (I mean come on, are you telling me that if instead of having a clear face, the trend was to have a face full of acne and to have as many freckles as possible then we wouldn't jump on that train? Hell no! We'd be looking up "DIY Acne Inhibitors", "10 ways to get blemishes", "Top ten freckle inducing creams" etc to get pimples.)

As if discriminating people based off their skin colour wasnt enough, now we have to judge people by tiny bumps on their face. Its ridiculous cause for some people, these bumps are only visible up close. I wonder when humans will outgrow this need to look good.
The whole idea of looking good is literally just that, an idea invented by other people. The worst part is that this idea will always change in the next few decades or so, so all your efforts? Straight to the drain it goes!

Remember when being skinny was a thing? Thigh gaps, size zeros and no booties? To the millions of women who worked their ass of trying to achieve the skinny frame? Too bad cause now being curvy, big booties and thunder thighs are a thing.

Similarly, clear skin is just a thing. Its just stuck around for a bit longer than other 'things' and it'll soon become a trend of the past. Say goodbye to all that acne medication you spent millions on once pizza face becomes a thing! I already see beauty gurus on instagram painting freckles on their face to achieve a 'that freckled look'. (Just type DIY make up freckles on YouTube and you'll see what I mean).

I think instead of telling people to change their skincare, diet and workout routine so as to help them with their acne, we should instead change our opinion. Just like beauty, ugliness also lies in the eyes of the beholder, so change the way you view things.
