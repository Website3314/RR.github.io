I like manners

Imagine you go on a date with a guy for the first time. You meet and he offers you a rose and compliments your fashion-sense. The date starts with him taking you to a fancy restaurant which brings a smile to your face.

The meal arrives and the waiter tells you to have a good time. You pick up your fork and knife and begin tearing into the meat, you pierce the separated piece with your fork and its juices spill out a little, you smell the fresh seasoning. Ss you bring the fork up to your mouth to chew as you look up to see your date.

He's eating in a manner that is comparative to the way prehistoric man used to eat; with his hands (not in the decent manner us indians eat, but a far more feral manner (such that even animals would judge him)) tearing the meat with both hands and porking it down his mouth gracelessly.

The same meat-juices that you found appetizing seconds back now seem disgusting as they spread across his mouth and drip down his chin. He grins at you sheepishly as he gobbles his meal and licks the gravy off his palms and fingers. He wipes his chin with the back of his hand and wipes his hands on his shirt, finishing off with a loud burp that cause everyone in the restaurant to stare at the two of you for a few seconds.

The trauma renders you unable to chew your food, you lose your appetite and thank him for the meal as you excuse yourself to pay a visit to the ladies' room where you barf (and proceed to the back exit of the restaurant to save whatever dignity you have left) never to see your date again.
You take up meditation to try and forget the memory and restore your faith in humanity.

Yeah, manners are important.
